# Originality & Plagiarism Guidelines

## Purpose & Scope
These guidelines direct the **Originality & Plagiarism Agent** to verify that all course content is original, legally compliant, properly attributed, and free from direct or paraphrased duplication. The agent also checks for AI-generated content that may have inadvertently introduced unattributed or fabricated material.

---

## Review Workflow
1. **Academic citation audit** – verify that all foundational research, terminology, and key algorithms/frameworks are cited with author names, paper titles, and publication identifiers (e.g., arXiv IDs).
2. **Originality scan** – check examples, code snippets, and text narratives against public tutorials, official SDK documentation, and competitor courses.
3. **AI-generated content check** – flag content that may have been generated by an LLM and introduced unverified claims, fabricated references, or paraphrased content from training data.
4. **Licensing & fair use review** – ensure any third-party code snippets, diagrams, or assets conform to fair-use guidelines and academic standards.
5. **Generate report** – produce a structured output using the schema defined below.

---

## Evaluation Criteria (5‑point scale)

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Academic Attributions** | Proper citation of foundational papers, authors, and arXiv references. |
| **Originality of Examples** | Use of unique scenarios, datasets, and character personas rather than generic or copied ones. |
| **Code Originality** | Custom-written boilerplate and scripts matching the specific variables and pacing of the course. |
| **Copyright Compliance** | No verbatim copying of official documentation or competitors' learning guides. |
| **Fair Use Integrity** | Educational snippets are limited in scope and clearly attributed. |
| **AI-Content Integrity** | No fabricated references, ghost citations, or unverifiable claims introduced by AI generation. |

---

## Scoring Rubric
- **5 – Excellent**: 100% original examples, perfect academic citations, clear license compliance, no AI-generated fabrications.
- **4 – Good**: Fully original content with minor missing citations or links that should be added.
- **3 – Satisfactory**: Authentic overall but uses highly generic examples or lacks academic grounding for key claims.
- **2 – Poor**: High volume of copy-pasted boilerplate or structural frameworks matching external sources too closely.
- **1 – Unacceptable**: Direct plagiarism, copyright infringement, or fabricated academic citations detected; reject immediately.

## Checklists

### Academic Citation Audit
```markdown
- [ ] All foundational techniques cite the original authors and papers:
      • Chain-of-Thought → Wei et al. (2022)
      • Few-Shot Learning → Brown et al. (2020)
      • Tree of Thoughts → Yao et al. (2023)
      • Lost in the Middle → Liu et al. (2023)
      • Nucleus Sampling / Top-P → Holtzman et al. (2020)
      • Zero-Shot Reasoning → Kojima et al. (2022)
      • LLM-as-Judge → Zheng et al. (2023)
      • Indirect Prompt Injection → Perez & Ribeiro (2022), Greshake et al. (2023)
- [ ] Citations include paper titles, not just author names.
- [ ] arXiv or DOI identifiers are provided where available.
- [ ] No "ghost citations" — every referenced paper actually exists and says what the course claims it says.
```

### Originality of Examples & Code
```markdown
- [ ] Scenario examples (e.g., meeting transcripts, support tickets, corporate case studies) are original creations — not copied from public datasets or competitor courses.
- [ ] Character names and company names in examples are unique (not "Acme Corp" from a well-known source).
- [ ] Code examples use custom variable names, comments, and application-specific settings — not verbatim SDK documentation snippets.
- [ ] Code snippets are newly written boilerplate tailored to the lesson's specific context, not generic copy-paste from cookbooks.
- [ ] Multi-provider code comparisons (OpenAI vs. Anthropic vs. Google) are structurally original, not lifted from comparison blog posts.
```

### Copyright & Documentation Compliance
```markdown
- [ ] No paragraphs copied from official documentation guides (e.g., OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide, Anthropic Cookbook, Google AI documentation).
- [ ] Concepts are summarized in the course's own words and structured within its own custom framework.
- [ ] Any diagrams, images, or graphics are original or properly licensed.
- [ ] Third-party code used under open-source licenses includes proper attribution.
```

### AI-Generated Content Integrity
```markdown
- [ ] No fabricated academic references (papers that don't exist, authors who didn't write them).
- [ ] No unverifiable statistics or claims (e.g., "studies show 90% improvement" without a source).
- [ ] No hallucinated product names, company names, or tool names.
- [ ] Prose does not contain suspiciously uniform paragraph structures across all lessons (a sign of bulk AI generation).
```

### Fair Use & Licensing
```markdown
- [ ] Brief code snippets from external sources fall within educational fair use.
- [ ] Paper titles and short quotes are used under academic citation norms.
- [ ] A licensing statement is included if the course itself is distributed (e.g., CC-BY, MIT).
- [ ] No full reproduction of copyrighted figures, tables, or extended passages.
```
